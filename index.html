About

Nicolas Morant began his technology journey at age 12, teaching himself HTML when the web was dominated by static pages and basic design. This sparked a passion that led to a diverse career spanning industries and continents. After earning a BBA and MBA, he transitioned from marketing to operations management, taking leadership roles at companies like Dainese and Corporate Stays, where he drove growth and efficiency.

To deepen his technical expertise, Nicolas earned a Master’s in Data Science from Harvard, refining his focus on AI, machine learning, and computer vision. This advanced knowledge directly influenced his work in non-invasive wildlife tracking.

In his current role as Technical Program Manager, Lead Engineer and Data Scientist at WildTrack, Nicolas leads AI-driven projects for wildlife protection. He actively contributes to software engineering, UI/UX design, and machine learning while ensuring efficient workflows through CI/CD and DevOps practices.

Nicolas’ journey from a self-taught coder to a recognized leader in conservation technology underscores his commitment to innovation, continuous learning, and applying technology to solve complex global challenges.
